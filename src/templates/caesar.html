Caesar Cipher (AKA Shift Cipher)

The Caesar cipher is one of the first known ciphers to be used and is elementary in nature.

Also known as the shift cipher, this cipher is known as a substitution cipher, such that each letter in the plaintext message is shifted a consistent amount down the alphabet.

For instance, the message "dog" with a shift of 1 would become "eph" as the letter 1 after 'd' is 'e', 1 after 'o' is 'p' and 1 after 'g' is 'h'
